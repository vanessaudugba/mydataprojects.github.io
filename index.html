GreenLeaf Stores
Mangement Dashboard Overview

GreenLeafStores is an early-stage online retailer, based in the United States of America, specializing in eco-friendly home products. In just its first year, the startup has seen rapid order growth but still relies on gut instinct and ad-hoc reports rather than a consistent, data-driven approach.

For this project, I used Microsoft Excel to analyze GreenLeaf Stores’ operational and sales data, transforming ad-hoc information into structured insights. The analysis supported a shift from intuition-based decision-making to a more consistent, data-driven approach, helping the business better understand performance trends and support informed strategic decisions.

The GreenLeaf Stores Management Dashboard provides a clear view of retail performance, highlighting revenue, profit, orders, and units sold. Built in Excel, it analyzes sales and profitability by product and state, tracks key KPIs, and visualizes trends, top customers, and regions showcasing strong data modeling, pivot tables, and dashboarding skills.

NextGen Tools
Performance and Strategy Overview

My Role & Impact

As the lead Business Analyst on this project, I was responsible for gathering requirements, analyzing data, and designing the dashboard. My work enabled NextGen Tools to gain a clearer understanding of their business performance, identify areas for improvement, and make informed strategic decisions that drive growth and efficiency.

Use of Excel for Dashboard & Insights

I designed the performance dashboard entirely in Excel, using advanced functions and visualization tools to convert raw datasets into executive-ready intelligence. I applied:

  • Data modeling and cleansing with Power Query and formulas to ensure accuracy and consistency.
  • Pivot Tables, dynamic charts, and slicers to enable interactive analysis of revenue, productivity, and customer trends.
  • KPI calculations and scenario analysis to compare actual performance against targets and forecast potential outcomes.

Through this approach, I was able to uncover patterns such as high-value customer segments, underperforming service lines, and process bottlenecks. These insights informed recommendations around resource allocation, pricing strategy, and process improvements, helping NextGen Tools make confident, evidence-based decisions.
